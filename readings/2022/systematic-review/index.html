    Literature Reviews in Innovation¶

    Introduction¶

    Literature reviews play an essential role in academic research to gather existing knowledge and to examine the state of a field. However, it is not easy to conduct systematic literature in a limited time when academic knowledge is expanding exponentially. Two weeks ago, I found a paper by Li Linnenluecke et al (2020), which shows how to use a package called Bibliometrix in R programming to assemble a broad overview of a field of interest to guide researcher's work.

    Instead of summarizing key ideas from this paper, I will try to conduct a short literature review by following the method those authors used in their paper. Here is the outline of this post:

    • literature search via Web of Science
    • export search results
    • clean the dataset
    • systematic review using R
    • extract topics

    Literature Search¶

    The basic search on Web of Science allows us to select journals. The search engine gives around 2 millions of results when you type innovation in the search bar and do not specify any field. In this post, I will focus on the top journals in business, economics and management (this means there is selection bias). 20 journals were selected and here is the distribution of relevant papers.

    Note * American Economic Review * Quarterly Journal of Economics * Journal of Financial Economics * Journal of Economic Literature * Journal of Finance * Econometrica * Academy of Management Journal * Academy of Management Review * Organization Science * Research Policy * Strategic Management Journal * Journal of Business Venturing * Organizational Research Methods * Journal of Management Studies * Journal of International Business Studies * British Journal of Management * Organization Studies * Technovation * Technological Forecasting and Social Change * Technology Analysis Strategic Management

    In the end, we got 11,489 journals. To refine my searching, I used the following query:

    • NOT Publishing Years: 2022 or 2021
    • NOT Document Types: Notes or Meeting Abstracts or Corrections or Reprints or Letters or Discussions or Software Reviews

    In then end, I got 10,000 (I did this on purpose as Web of Science only enables citation analysis when the number of articles is less than 10,000). The general trend is presented in the following figure and top 20 most cited papers are listed in the table below.
