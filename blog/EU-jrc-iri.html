Firm Ownership and The Complexity of Our Economy

Multinational corporations have huge influence on our daily life with their global presence; in this post, I will use a dataset from Orbis to trace their footprints.

data-science, dataset, python, r, business-analytics, string-matching

Published

26 February 2023

EU Joint Research Center (JRC) published a report called EU Industrial R&D Investment Scoreboard, which analyzes the world top 2500 R&D investors and EU top 1000 R&D investors (Grassano et al., 2021). In this report I learned that the world top 2500 R&D investors with headquarters in 39 countries, have more than 800k subsidiaries all over the world.

I was curious how and where they got the number as it shows the strong presence of multinational corporations (MNCs) globally. In this post, I will find out who those MNCs and subsidiaries are and where they locate. I will use R to do business analytics and code will be shared here for anyone who wants to use it as a case study of learning R (or data.table package).

We will now visualize the distribution of firms in terms of industry.

Figure 1. Histogram of number of employees and its log transformation; it is important to notice that the left panel figure is right skewed.

One of the stylized facts about firm level dataset is that many univariate random variables have the right skewed distribution and the ‘bell-shaped’ curve was only visible after doing logarithm transformation.

Figure 2. Correlation matrix shows that there is a strong correlation between R&D and net sales.

Figure 2 shows the strong correlation exists in a group of variables: number of employees, R&D expenditure, revenue (net sales), capital expenditure, and profit. The story can be simplified as a narratives: the high R&D spending gives the market power of a firm, which brings higher revenue; then the firm could invest more on R&D, upgrade their assets and of course hire more talents.

Top 1000 EU R&D investors

In this section, I will search for subsidiaries for top 1000 EU R&D investors. Figure 3 shows that Germany is the leader of innovation in the EU. I will search for firms’ subsidiaries based on the rank in the Figure 3.

Figure 3. Top EU R&D investors distribution across countries, which shows Germany is the leading country of innovation in the EU.

Many papers have already tried to find out the subsidiaries of MNCs (Ribeiro et al., 2010; Kalemli-Ozcan et al., 2015; Clò et al., 2020; Grassano et al., 2021), I will try to visualize the landscape of MNCs’ subsidiaries.

Figure 4 shows that MNCs are more involved in the retail industry have the higher number of subsidiaries as they need a big ‘network’ to reach to their customers.

Figure 4. Top 20 MNCs in terms of the number of their subsidiaries, the color indicates the number of their shareholders.
